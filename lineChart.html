Key Takeaways

The line chart shows different trends in microplastic and macroplastic leakage throughout time. While both groups are on the rise, macroplastics fluctuate more, which is most likely due to external causes like as policy changes or cleanup activities. Microplastic leakage, on the other hand, increases steadily and consistently, indicating a longer-term and less fluctuating development trend.

Graph Justification:

  • Line charts are good for showing trends over time, emphasising the overall direction and pace of change for each category.
  • Comparing microplastics and macroplastics on the same graph makes it simpler to examine their growth patterns and determine which offers a greater immediate or long-term environmental risk.
  • Smooth lines highlight the data's continuity, emphasising the ongoing growth in plastic pollution and the huge environmental burden it represents.
