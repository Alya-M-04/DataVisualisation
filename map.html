Key Takeaways

The map visualisation offers a strong, user-friendly method of examining plastic leakage statistics across different OECD regions, revealing differences in plastic waste trends by region. Users may easily compare the amount and type of waste in each region by seeing the distribution of microplastics and macroplastics across regions like OECD Europe, OECD Asia, and OECD Eurasia. This enables greater understanding. In addition to being a useful tool for communication, the map gives stakeholders an opportunity to track differences in locations and trends over time.

Graph Justification:

  • Geographic Clarity: By presenting complicated facts in a visually engaging way, the map helps the identification of geographical differences.
  • Data Simplification: Large datasets may be presented more easily using the map format, especially to non-technical audiences.
  • Regional Comparisons: Comparing plastic leakage across locations is simple for users.
  • Dynamic Insights: By enabling users to monitor trends over time, the year filter improves the map's efficiency.
  • Targeted Actions: Helpful in identifying places with greater concentrations of certain plastic kinds, allowing for more focused waste reduction activities.
